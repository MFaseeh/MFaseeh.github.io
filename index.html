About

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Electronic Engineering at Jeju National University, South Korea, working under the supervision of Prof. Do Hyeun Kim, supported by the Brain Korea (BK21) fully-funded scholarship.

My research sits at the intersection of deep learning and applied sciences — developing AI-driven methods for computer vision tasks like object detection, monocular depth estimation, and long-range tracking, alongside NLP applications such as text similarity, sarcasm detection, and essay scoring.

A significant strand of my work extends into Material Informatics, where I apply Transformer and AutoML-based inverse learning frameworks for ceramic material design and property prediction — including BaTiO₃-based ferroelectric ceramics.

With 23 published and accepted papers across IEEE, Elsevier, Springer, Wiley, MDPI, Taylor & Francis, and PLOS ONE, I bring a track record of high-impact research. Prior to my PhD, I spent 6+ years in academia as a lecturer, lab engineer, and vice principal across institutions in Pakistan.

Research Projects

01
Computer Vision Object Detection Depth Estimation YOLOv11 Custom Dataset
Custom Dataset Development & Real-Time Long-Range Object Detection Using YOLOv11 with Depth Estimation
Developed DFar300, a custom dataset enabling object detection at distances up to 300 meters, addressing the absence of long-range benchmarks in the field. Implemented a YOLOv11-based detection pipeline integrated with depth estimation to deliver accurate real-time object localization and distance measurement.
02
Material Informatics NLP Feature Fusion BaTiO₃ Predictive Modeling
AI-Driven Material Property Prediction via Multi-Source Feature Fusion and NLP-Enhanced Learning
Engineered a predictive model for BaTiO₃ material properties by fusing computational simulations with NLP-derived datasets to enrich feature representation. Applied advanced feature selection and fusion techniques within a forward learning framework to improve prediction accuracy across diverse material components and processing conditions.
03
Inverse Learning Material Informatics Feature Engineering BaTiO₃ Attention Mechanism
Multi-Strategy Feature Fusion and Inverse Learning with Post-Processing Optimization for BaTiO₃ Material Property Prediction
Designed a comprehensive inverse learning pipeline incorporating mathematical, statistical, and physics-informed feature transformations to predict BaTiO₃ material properties. Leveraged multiple fusion strategies — including weighted, hierarchical, attention-based, and block-wise fusion — alongside post-processing optimization to significantly enhance model performance and output reliability.
