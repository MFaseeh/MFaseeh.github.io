About Me
Bridging AI with
Real-World Impact

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Electronic Engineering at Jeju National University, South Korea, working under the supervision of Prof. Do Hyeun Kim. My research sits at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and materials science — developing AI-driven methods that accelerate scientific discovery.

With over 23 published and accepted papers in top-tier journals including IEEE Access, Elsevier, Springer, and Wiley, I bring a strong track record of translating research into impact. My work spans object detection, depth estimation, NLP, and inverse learning for ceramic material design.

Prior to my PhD, I spent 6+ years in academia as a lecturer, lab engineer, and vice principal — giving me a unique combination of deep technical expertise and real-world teaching experience.
